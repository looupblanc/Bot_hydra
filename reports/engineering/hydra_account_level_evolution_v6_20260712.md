# HYDRA Account-Level Evolution & Mechanism Grammar V6

## Immutable engineering task

- Scientific objective: test whether behaviorally distinct, positive-net V5
  components can reach the Topstep 150K target more reliably when replayed on
  one chronological shared account, and reopen structural novelty with a
  materially new multi-condition/transition grammar.
- Required targeted evolution: devote a bounded population to one-dimensional
  target-velocity mutations of the strongest exact components, compare every
  parent and child on identical block-aware episode starts, prohibit blind
  contract multiplication, and admit a child only when target progress,
  passage, consistency, opportunity density, or cost-adjusted economics improve
  without an unacceptable MLL deterioration or behavioral duplication.
- Required XFA allocation: evaluate a bounded set of the strongest static
  baskets as separately frozen, globally serial XFA policies so concurrent-MAE
  ambiguity cannot inflate payout survival; report payout cycles, qualifying
  behavior, MLL survival, post-payout survival and no inherited Combine status.
- Required behavior: build an immutable component bank; evolve individual,
  static-basket, and deterministic-controller populations; merge actual event
  timelines; enforce one target, one MLL, shared limits, conflicts, costs,
  consistency and session rules; compare policies on identical episode starts;
  require controller improvements to clear paired economic and one-sided sign
  evidence on independent episode blocks against both the static basket and
  matched random router; keep overlapping starts descriptive rather than
  treating them as independent evidence; require 48-start promotion before a
  screening basket/controller becomes a headline elite; use stable structural
  basket identities across generations and exclude prior basket fingerprints;
  persist populations and automatically queue the next V6 generation.
- Allowed paths: `hydra/account_policy/`,
  `hydra/research/mechanism_grammar_v6.py`,
  `hydra/research/account_level_evolution_v6.py`, mission experiment routing,
  the V6 command, tests, and this report.
- Protected paths: governance kernel, Q4 data and manifests, data-role ledger
  semantics, raw market data, registry and mission databases, broker/order
  integrations, secrets.
- Data boundary: development/falsification data ending before 2024-10-01.
  Q4 reuse, paid data, network access, live trading and outbound orders are
  prohibited.
- Acceptance tests: shared chronological replay; shared target/MLL/consistency/
  contract limits; deterministic past-only controller; profit/loss guards;
  identical comparison starts; novelty/freeze logic; persistent mission queue
  refill; one writer; deterministic replay; full pytest; no-lookahead;
  compileall; DB integrity; governance, budget and secret checks.
- Rollback conditions: any future-state dependency, summary-statistic basket
  approximation, multiple writer, protected data access, non-determinism,
  contract/session regression, or persistent scheduler regression.
- Expected decision information value: high. V5 has six comparable generations
  with unchanged 8.33% maximum pass rate, novelty below 1%, repeated parents and
  zero Combine elites. Account-level coordination and a new mechanism grammar
  directly test the two remaining high-value explanations: insufficient
  opportunity coverage and uncoordinated shared risk.
